Marriage as a Commodity

作品介绍

Marriage as a Commodity This project centers on the social phenomenon of contemporary Chinese matchmaking, taking the common matchmakingcorners in urban parks as the core research and creative entry point. In thesecorners, parents display their children's age, educational background, income status, and other information on paper for public viewing, forming a kind of labeled human market. I created a wedding invitation to tell the story of a man and a woman who were controlled by feudal superstition and eventually got married.
